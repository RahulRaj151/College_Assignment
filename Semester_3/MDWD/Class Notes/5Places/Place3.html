Visakhapatnam

Visakhapatnam, also called Vizag, is the fourth largest city in India located on the east coast of Andhra Pradesh. It has a scenic coastline and beaches like RK Beach and Yarada Beach. Visakhapatnam derives its name from the ancient Visakha Mahavinayak temple located in the city. It has a large natural harbor strategically located on the shipping lanes between East Asia and West Asia. This makes it a major port city. Visakhapatnam has a developing industrial base, especially related to steel and petrochemicals. It houses the headquarters of the Eastern Naval Command of the Indian Navy. The city attracts tourists for its picturesque beaches, hills like Kailasagiri and Dolphin's Nose overlooking the sea. Visakhapatnam has several historic churches and lighthouses built by the British. It has a cosmopolitan culture with people from different parts of India settling here.
